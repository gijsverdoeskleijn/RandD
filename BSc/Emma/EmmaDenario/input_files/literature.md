The proposed idea can be considered **novel**.

The novelty stems from the specific combination of the observational technique and the scientific objective. While components of the idea exist individually in the literature, their integration as proposed appears to be unprecedented.

The core of the idea is to use stellar occultation observations of known asteroids with high perihelion precession to improve constraints on perihelion precession, and subsequently use these improvements to constrain the Parametric Post Newtonian (PPN) formalism parameter beta and the Sun’s gravitational quadrupole moment J_2,Sun.
